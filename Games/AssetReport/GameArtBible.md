## Vision

*Asset Report* is a **kingdom-management game about the people who do the
paperwork of heroism**. While knights raise their swords against dragons and a
golden god watches from the clouds, **you** are the clerk at the desk — stamping
quest approvals, balancing the treasury, itemizing the receipts, and quietly
keeping an entire realm of adventurers solvent.

The player fantasy is **heroic administration**: the satisfying, slightly absurd
power of being the indispensable functionary behind the legend. You never swing
the sword. You *approve the requisition for the sword* — and the realm runs
because your ledgers balance.

Players should feel **competent, wry, and quietly proud**. The mood is the warm
exhaustion of a job well done: the glory is real and visible, but it is happening
*because of* the unglamorous work in the foreground. The art must make the player
love both halves — the splendor of the kingdom **and** the dignity of the desk.

---

## Core Identity

- **Genre:** kingdom / settlement management with a layer of quest-and-economy
  administration.
- **Identity in one line:** *heroic bureaucratic fantasy* — civilized,
  organized high fantasy seen from behind the clerk's desk.
- **The defining tension:** **epic glory vs. mundane paperwork.** Every key
  visual holds both at once. The heroes, gods, and dragons are rendered with
  total sincerity and grandeur; the comedy and warmth come from *framing* them
  against ledgers, stamps, abacuses, and a tired administrator — never from
  making the fantasy itself look silly.
- **Adjacent reference points:** kingdom-builder map of settlements and
  resources; civil-service comedy of forms and approvals; classic high-fantasy
  hero roster. All three coexist.

This is **not** a war game, a dungeon crawler, or a grimdark survival sim. It is
**civilized fantasy** — a functioning realm with a treasury, a guild office, a
quest board, and a very long queue of expense forms.

---

## Tone

- **Wry, affectionate comedy with genuine grandeur underneath.** The joke is the
  *juxtaposition* (a glowing god-king in the sky, a **DENIED** stamp on the
  desk), not slapstick. Deadpan, dry, bureaucratic humor.
- **Optimism and humanity.** The clerk is sympathetic and dignified — overworked,
  ink-stained, but never a punching bag. The realm is prosperous and worth
  running.
- **Earned wonder.** When the heroes and the divine appear, they are awe-inspiring
  for real. The game cashes the awe so the desk-work means something.
- **Warmth over edge.** No grimdark, no cynicism, no cruelty. Even the denied
  quests are funny, not bleak.
- **Politics as paperwork.** The intrigue of the realm is expressed through
  documents, seals, approvals, and budgets rather than blood.

Emotional register, in order of dominance: **warm wit → quiet pride → measured
wonder → gentle drama.**

## World

- **Technology level:** pre-industrial medieval. Quills, parchment, wax seals,
  abacuses, ledgers, windmills, water-driven forges, hand carts, timber cranes.
  No gunpowder, no clockwork machines, no steampunk.
- **Magic level:** real but **rare and reserved**. The divine and the arcane
  genuinely glow (the sky-god radiates, a mage's staff lights) — but magic is the
  exception, not the texture of daily life. Per the style guide: **only magic
  emits light; mundane materials never glow.** Most of the world is honest
  stone, wood, and parchment.
- **Architecture:** a civilized human kingdom. Pale weathered stone keeps with
  crenellations; blue conical and domed roofs with gold finials on civic and
  landmark buildings; timber-and-plaster on humble working buildings; red-tiled
  roofs on the barracks; striped awnings over market stalls. Banners everywhere.
- **Climate:** temperate and golden. The world lives at a perpetual warm
  late-afternoon — god-rays, dust in the light, long warm shadows.
- **Culture & civilization:** an ordered realm of **Order/human-kingdom**
  character (royal blue + warm gold, per the style guide's faction keys). It has
  guilds, a treasury, a civil service, a quest economy, and clear social ranks
  from clerk to king.
- **Infrastructure:** roads of settlements and resource sites linked on a map;
  lumber camps, quarries, mines, and farms feeding forges, academies, and
  markets. The kingdom is a supply chain.
- **Trade:** a coin economy denominated in **gold pieces (gp)**, run through
  markets and a central treasury. Quest payouts, expense reimbursements, and
  building costs all settle in gp.
- **Religion:** a radiant sky-pantheon — a benevolent, luminous god-king figure
  in the clouds, served on the ground by temples and a divine order. Faith is a
  source of light, literally and thematically.
- **Daily life:** the realm is *busy*. Heroes file quests, merchants haggle,
  builders raise keeps — and somewhere a clerk reconciles all of it on paper.

## Architecture

Recurring architectural language to keep every new building on-brand:

- **Stone:** pale, warm, weathered ashlar and rubble. Crenellated tops on keeps
  and walls. Matte and chalky (style-guide material rules apply). Honest wear,
  never grime.
- **Roofs:** the signature shapes. **Blue** conical spires and domes with **gold
  finials** mark civic, royal, and scholarly buildings (Home Keep, Grand Citadel,
  Settlement, Academy). **Red tile** marks the military (Barracks). Practical
  buildings get plain grey-slate or timber roofs.
- **Wood:** oiled timber framing, plaster infill, scaffolding, cranes, carts,
  palisades on humble and working structures.
- **Scale & progression:** humble → grand reads through silhouette mass and
  ornament. A lone Settlement watchpost grows into the multi-towered Grand
  Citadel; a Lumber Camp lean-to is dwarfed by the great domed Academy. More
  importance = taller, more towers, more banners, more gold.
- **Construction cues:** cranes, scaffolds, fresh-cut timber, and cut-stone
  blocks signal a realm actively being built.
- **Districts / families:** buildings cluster by function (see
  [`Buildings.md`](Buildings.md)) — civic core, resource sites, military line,
  economy, and the divine — each with its own roof color, banner, and badge.
- **Banners & heraldry:** blue-and-gold pennants on player/civic structures; red
  heraldic banners on military ones. Banners are how a building announces its
  allegiance and function at a glance.

## Buildings

Buildings are the heart of the map and fall into **families**. Do not pre-design
every building — design *to the family system* so new buildings slot in. (Full
spec: [`Buildings.md`](Buildings.md).)

| Family | Examples (from references) | Reads as |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Civic / Player core** | Home Keep, Grand Citadel, Settlement | Blue-and-gold, stone, crenellations, the player's identity |
| **Resource extraction** | Lumber Camp, Quarry, Farmland, Iron Mine, Gold Mine | Working, timber-and-stone, tools and raw material on show |
| **Research / Craft** | Academy, Forge | Domed scholarship vs. fire-lit industry |
| **Military / Defense** | Barracks, Archer Tower, Wall | Red banners, drilled and fortified |
| **Economy** | Market | Striped awnings, stalls, goods, coin |
| **Divine** | Temple | Radiant, the only building that emits light |

Each building is delivered as a **map node**: a themed isometric scene sitting in
the consistent gold **Base Ring**, with a circular **Top Badge** identifying its
type and a warm **rim light**. The node anatomy is canonical — see
[`Buildings.md`](Buildings.md) and [`Icons.md`](Icons.md).

## Resources

The economy is medieval and tangible. Likely resource set (full visual spec in
[`Resources.md`](Resources.md)):

- **Wood** — stacked oiled logs (Lumber Camp).
- **Stone** — cut grey blocks (Quarry).
- **Food / Grain** — wheat sheaves, the windmill harvest (Farmland).
- **Iron / Ore** — raw blue-grey ore, ingots (Iron Mine).
- **Gold (gp)** — coins and nuggets; the realm's currency and the lifeblood of
  the ledger (Gold Mine, Market, Treasury).
- **Knowledge / Research** — books, scrolls, the open tome (Academy).
- **Faith / Divine favor** — radiant star/light (Temple) — the one *emissive*
  resource.
- **Influence / Approval** — the bureaucratic meta-resource: stamps, seals,
  signatures, processed paperwork. Unique to this game and central to its
  identity.

Resource icons live inside circular gold badges and must read at a glance and in
grayscale. Gold is the connective tissue: it threads through the economy, the
quest payouts, and the UI.

## Icon System

Icons carry meaning before any text is read. (Canonical spec:
[`Icons.md`](Icons.md).)

- **Hierarchy:** building map-node > type badge > resource/UI icon. Each tier has
  a consistent size and frame so the player learns the visual grammar once.
- **Badge placement:** the building **Top Badge** sits centered at the top of the
  Base Ring, slightly overlapping it — the node's "type tag."
- **Frames:** two canonical frames. A **circular gold ring** for resources,
  civic, economy, and most node badges; a **heraldic banner-shield** (often red)
  for military and defense. Frame shape itself encodes category.
- **Color coding:** keyed to the style guide's faction/resource keys — blue+gold
  for civic/Order, red for military, gold-neutral for resources, warm radiant for
  divine.
- **Silhouettes:** one concept per icon, bold and legible at ~32px, identifiable
  as a black silhouette alone (a hammer, a wheat sheaf, a tome, crossed swords, a
  pair of scales).
- **Scale & readability:** interior detail only where it aids recognition;
  everything must survive shrinking to a node badge or a resource bar pip.

## UI Philosophy

The UI is the game's **secret main character** — it is the desk, the ledger, the
stamp. It should feel like handling real bureaucratic objects of a fantasy
kingdom. (Full spec: [`UI.md`](UI.md).) Describe, do not implement:

- **Buttons:** like wax-sealed approvals, stamped tokens, or engraved brass
  plates — warm, tactile, with a satisfying "official" weight.
- **Frames & panels:** parchment sheets, ledger pages, pinned forms, and
  gold-engraved plates (the title treatment is the north star).
- **Windows:** clipboards, open ledgers, document trays — the screen is a desk
  surface.
- **Scrolls:** torn-edge parchment banners for subtitles and minor headings.
- **Notifications:** stamps hitting paper (**APPROVED** / **DENIED**), seals
  pressing, new forms landing in the "IN" tray.
- **Badges:** wax seals and heraldic emblems mark status, rank, and faction.
- **Resource bars:** a row of gold-rimmed resource icons with counts — the
  running balance of the realm.
- **Menus & tooltips:** filed, indexed, labeled — the look of an exquisitely
  organized civil service.

Mood: **warm, official, hand-made, lightly worn.** Authoritative but never cold;
ornate but always legible.

- **Buildings (map nodes)** — the Base-Ring building set; the largest collection.
- **Type badges** — node-type identifier icons.
- **Resource icons** — the economy set.
- **UI icons** — actions, statuses, stamps, navigation.
- **Characters** — heroes (knight, mage, ranger, fighter), and the civil service

- **Creatures** — dragons and the monsters that quests are filed against.
- **Documents** — ledgers, quest forms, expense reports, seals, stamps (a
  category almost unique to this game).
- **Loading screens / divine moments** — radiant sky-god and faith imagery.

## Visual Storytelling

- **Visuals communicate gameplay through the glory/paperwork split.** Foreground
  = your job (desk, documents, decisions); background = the consequence (heroes,
  realm, gods). Composition itself teaches "your small actions fund the legend."
- **Buildings communicate function** through roof color, banner, badge, and the
  props in their scene: a forge glows with fire and shows an anvil; an academy
  shows a dome and an open book; a market shows awnings and goods. Read the
  building, know the job.
- **Icons communicate progression** through ornament and scale: a humble
  Settlement vs. a many-towered Grand Citadel; a plain badge vs. a richer one.
  Bigger, taller, more gold = more advanced.
- **Color communicates meaning** via the style guide's keys: **blue + gold =
  civic/player/Order**, **red = military/defense**, **gold-neutral = resource /
  economy / currency**, **radiant warm light = divine/faith**, and **grey-brown
  parchment = the neutral working world** where the paperwork lives.
- **Light communicates value:** the warmer and brighter, the more glorious. The
  desk is cooler and more muted; the heroes and the god are bathed in the warm
  key light. Brightness is reverence.

## Things To Avoid

Visual directions that do **not** belong in *Asset Report*:

- **Grimdark, gore, horror, or bleakness.** This realm is prosperous and warm.
- **Zany cartoon / slapstick rendering.** The humor is deadpan and framed; the
  fantasy is drawn straight. No goofy proportions, no rubber-hose, no meme art.
- **Sci-fi, steampunk, clockwork, gunpowder.** Pre-industrial medieval only.
- **Modern office clichés** (staplers, fluorescent light, cubicles, sticky
  notes). The bureaucracy is **fantasy-medieval** — quills, wax, ledgers, abacus.
- **Neon, rainbow, oversaturation, photoreal/3D-render look.** (Style-guide hard
  limits.)
- **Glowing mundane objects.** Only the divine and arcane emit light.
- **Cluttered, noisy compositions** that bury the focal point. Even the busy
  desk must have one clear subject and room to breathe.
- **Cold, sterile, corporate minimalism.** Everything is warm, hand-tooled, and
  worn-in.

## Future Expansion

Logical, on-brand collections to grow into:

- **The Civil Service roster** — clerks, the Treasurer, the Quartermaster, the
  Auditor, the Tax Collector, the Royal Scribe: heroes of administration.
- **Document & stationery set** — quest forms, expense reports, audit notices,
  writs, royal decrees, seals, and a library of stamps (APPROVED, DENIED,
  PENDING, OVERDUE).
- **Building upgrade tiers** — visual progression art for each building family
  (Settlement → Town → Citadel), holding the family's roof/banner identity.
- **Quest creatures & encounters** — the dragons, goblins, and bandits that
  appear as line items on the ledger.
- **Faction variants** — should other realms appear, render them with the style
  guide's Wild (green/bone) and Arcane (violet/cyan) shape-and-color keys, still
  filtered through the civilized, bureaucratic lens.
- **Seasonal / event key art** — audit season, tax day, the founding festival —
  reusing the glory-vs-paperwork splash template.
- **Divine & faith collection** — the sky-god pantheon, temple interiors,
  blessings and miracles (the emissive set).
