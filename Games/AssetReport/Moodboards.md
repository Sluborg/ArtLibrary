# Asset Report — Moodboards (in words)

> Visual inspiration described in **words**, not image collections. This captures
> the recurring themes and feelings the art should evoke, so a fresh Lubot chat
> can aim at the right target before generating. Supports
> [`GameArtBible.md`](GameArtBible.md); rendering rules live in the
> [style guide](../../StyleGuides/Fantasy-Strategy-v1.md).

## The one image that explains everything

A **tired, ink-stained clerk in round glasses** holds up a clipboard of quest
approvals — a red **DENIED** stamp on one line, gold coins beside the payouts, an
abacus and a wax seal on the desk. **Behind and above them**, the heavens open: a
luminous **god-king** in the clouds throws golden light over four heroes — a
knight raising a sword, a blue mage, a green ranger, an armored shield-bearer —
while a red dragon wheels over a vast battlefield of banners.

That single frame is the game: **the glory is real, and someone has to do its
paperwork.** Every moodboard below is a facet of that contrast.

## Recurring themes

### 1. Glory above, ledger below
A vertical sandwich: epic, warm, god-lit fantasy at the top of the frame;
cooler, muted, document-strewn desk at the bottom. The eye travels from the
heroes down to the hand holding the form. Splash art and key moments live here.

### 2. The warm golden hour of a prosperous realm
The whole world sits in a perpetual late-afternoon: god-rays through cloud,
dust in the light, long warm shadows, gold on stone. Optimistic, civilized,
never bleak. This is the *temperature* of the game.

### 3. The dignified desk of a fantasy civil service
Quills, inkwells, wax seals, ledgers, clipboards, abacuses, filing trays marked
**IN** / **PENDING**, pinned reminders ("ITEMIZE EVERYTHING"). Hand-made,
worn-in, lovingly organized. Bureaucracy rendered with affection, not contempt.

### 4. The map as a board of gold-ringed nodes
A dark velvet field studded with **gold rings**, each holding a glowing little
building world — keeps, mines, markets, temples — each tagged with a small badge,
each catching the same warm rim light. A kingdom you read at a glance, like
pieces laid out on a commander's table.

### 5. Heraldry and allegiance
Banners everywhere: **blue-and-gold** for the player's realm, **red** for the
military, gold sigils and stars on cloth. Wax seals and crests mark who owns
what. The realm announces itself in pennants.

### 6. Reserved, reverent magic
Light means meaning. The **god-king and the temple glow**; a mage's staff glows;
nothing mundane does. When light appears, it is sacred or arcane and it is the
brightest thing in frame. Wonder is rationed so it stays wonderful.

### 7. Deadpan comedy through juxtaposition
The humor is never in the rendering — it is in the **pairing**: a world-ending
dragon reduced to a line item; a god's blessing requiring a counter-signature; a
hero's reward stamped **DENIED** for a missing receipt. Draw the fantasy
straight; let the framing be funny.

## Texture & material feel

Aged parchment, oiled timber, chalky weathered stone, low-gloss forged gold and
steel, soft matte banners, red wax, black ink, brass clips, pressed-leaf laurel.
Everything has honest wear — chips, patina, ink stains — but stays clean and
deliberate. Nothing is glossy, plastic, neon, or sterile.

## Emotional keywords

`heroic` · `administrative` · `warm` · `wry` · `dignified` · `golden-hour` ·
`civilized` · `ledger-and-banner` · `reverent` · `worn-in` · `competent` ·
`measured wonder` · `quietly proud`

## Anti-moodboard (what it must never feel like)

Grimdark battlefields of mud and blood · zany cartoon mascots · sterile modern
office cubicles · neon sci-fi HUDs · photoreal 3D renders · cluttered noisy
fan-art · cynical or mean-spirited satire. If a reference feels like any of
these, it is the wrong target.
